What is Postpartum Hemorrhage?

Postpartum hemorrhage (PPH) is excessive bleeding following childbirth. It is a serious medical emergency that requires immediate intervention to save the motherâ€™s life.

About Postpartum Hemorrhage

PPH occurs when a mother loses more than 500ml of blood after vaginal delivery or more than 1000ml after a cesarean section.

Causes:

  • Uterine atony (failure of the uterus to contract)
  • Retained placenta
  • Genital tract trauma
  • Clotting disorders

Prevention:

Active management of the third stage of labor, proper monitoring, and access to skilled birth attendants can reduce PPH risks significantly.
